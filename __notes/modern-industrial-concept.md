# Zennyx Modern Industrial Concept

Primary direction: `/`

Reference snapshot: `/concept-industrial`

The concept was first isolated for comparison, then promoted into the complete public site direction. The snapshot route remains private and noindexed as a reference while the primary homepage and inner pages use the same system.

## Interpretation

Modern industrial means a visible creative production system: strong rails, exposed borders, cut-corner markers, utilitarian metadata, cold neutral material colors, and safety amber used as a signal.

It does not mean factory role-play. The concept deliberately avoids gears, hazard-stripe decoration, fake machinery, gauges, manufacturing language, and decorative technical schematics.

## Creative game studio anchors

- The current game remains the dominant visual object.
- Production information describes real gameplay work and milestones.
- Devlogs remain a human record, not a performance dashboard.
- Andara and local urban texture provide the world-facing identity.
- Fajar and Faiz are shown as a small creative crew with shared responsibility.

## Anti-AI and anti-SaaS guardrails

- No glassmorphism, glow, gradient mesh, or floating 3D objects.
- No pill navigation or rounded card system.
- No fake KPIs, analytics, percentages, or feature dashboards.
- No bento layout assembled from interchangeable cards.
- No generic startup CTA language.

## Separation from Fajar Works

- Cold steel-grey foundation instead of warm cream.
- Safety amber and deep studio green instead of rust-led editorial color.
- Project window and production rail instead of portfolio showcase frames.
- Mixed-case utility typography instead of oversized uppercase portfolio claims.
- Game build, playtest priorities, world, and release structure instead of skills and case studies.
